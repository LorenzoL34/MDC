Alfajores

Learn how to create Argentinian authentic alfajores cookies, that perfectly blend sweet and salty.

Ingredients

Cookie Dough
  • 1½ cups Cornstarch
  • 1¼ cups All-Purpose Flour
  • ½ tsp Baking Powder
  • ¼ tsp Salt
  • ¾ cup Salted Butter
  • ¾ cup Granulated Sugar
  • 3 Egg Yolks
  • Zest Of 1 Lemon
  • 1 tsp Vanilla Extract
  • 1 tbsp Rum
Filling and Finish
  • 2 cups Dulce de Leche
  • 1 cup Unsweetened Shredded Coconut

Instructions

1. Mix Dry Ingredients
In a bowl, whisk together:
  • Cornstarch
  • Flour
  • Baking Powder
  • Salt
Set Aside.
2. Cream Butter And Sugar
  1. In a large bowl, beat the soft butter and sugar until pale and creamy (2-3) minutes
  2. Add the egg yolks, one at a time, mixing gently
  3. Mix In:
  • Lemon Zest
  • Vanilla
  • Rum
3. Form The Dough
  1. Add the dry ingredients gradually
  2. Mix just until combined, do not knead. The dough should be soft
  3. Flatten into a disk, wrap, and refrigerate 30-45 minutes.
4. Roll And Cut
  1. Preheat oven to 170°C/ 340°F
  2. Roll dough between parchment to 6-7mm (1¼ inch) thick.
  3. Place on lined baking sheets, slightly spaced.
5. Bake
  1. Bake for 10-12 minutes
  2. The cookies should stay very pale-no browning.
  3. Cool completely before filling.
6. Fill And Finish
  1. Spread dulce de leche generously on one cookie.
  2. Sandwich with another.
  3. Roll the edges in shredded coconut.
Pro Argentinian Tips
  • Use dulce de leche repostero, regular dulce de leche will ooze.
  • Let alfajores rest 12-24 hours in a closed container before serving. (They softern and taste even better).
